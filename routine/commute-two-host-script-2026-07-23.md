## SEGMENT SIX — ONE GOOD THING
ALEX: One good thing, and it's a genuine wonder-of-the-world story. Scientists have formally described a brand-new species of monkey deep in the Congo Basin.
SAM: A whole new monkey. In 2026.
ALEX: A whole new monkey. It's called the likweli — scientific name Colobus congoensis — a small, black, long-tailed monkey with striking orange-cream patches on its face and white markings under its tail. It lives in lowland rainforest between the Lomami and Lilo rivers in the Democratic Republic of Congo.
SAM: How rare is it to describe a new primate?
ALEX: This is only the fifth new African monkey species described in 75 years — and it's the first primate ever named for the DRC itself. The team, led by John Hart, Junior Amboko, and Kate Detwiler, logged 114 field observations before publishing in the journal PLOS One in mid-July. It's a discovery, not a rediscovery.
