## ENERGY / DATA CENTERS / UTILITIES (~5 min)
The collision story of 2026: **AI data centers vs. an already-strained grid.**
- In **June 2026 the US Department of Energy launched "Agora,"** a test platform that simulates how hyperscale AI campuses behave when they plug into the grid — modeling the volatile, high-density loads that are reshaping utility planning.
- Why it matters: GPU clusters can ramp **from near-idle to full power in seconds**, behaving less like a normal data center and more like a heavy industrial load that slams the grid instantly.
- The shortfall is concrete: **PJM Interconnection** — the biggest US grid operator, 65M+ people across 13 states — projects it will be **6 gigawatts short** of its reliability requirement in 2027. The independent market monitor calls it "crisis stage right now... PJM has never been this short."
- The demand curve: Bloom Energy projects US data-center power demand **nearly doubles from ~80 GW (2025) to ~150 GW (2028)** — like bolting on a country the size of Spain in three years.
- The kitchen-table kicker: this is starting to show up on **household electric bills**, which turns a tech story into a political one. Watch utilities, independent power producers, gas turbine makers, and anything nuclear/SMR-adjacent.

## VOCAB OF THE DAY (~5 min)

### Mandarin (for the active learner — say each twice, honor the tones)

**1. 谈判 — tánpàn** [tahn-PAHN] (tán = rising 2nd tone, pàn = falling 4th tone)
- **Meaning:** negotiation / to negotiate (formal talks between parties).
- **Tie-in:** the US–Iran 谈判 that just produced the ceasefire framework.
- **Example:** 两国正在进行谈判。 — *Liǎng guó zhèngzài jìnxíng tánpàn.* [lyahng gwor jung-dzai jin-shing tahn-pahn] — "The two countries are holding negotiations."

**2. 电网 — diànwǎng** [dyen-WAHNG] (diàn = falling 4th tone, wǎng = dipping 3rd tone)
- **Meaning:** power grid / electricity grid. (电 diàn = electricity, 网 wǎng = net/web.)
- **Tie-in:** the AI-data-center strain on the 电网 in today's energy segment.
- **Example:** 数据中心给电网带来很大压力。 — *Shùjù zhōngxīn gěi diànwǎng dàilái hěn dà yālì.* [shoo-joo joong-shin gay dyen-wahng dye-lye hun dah yah-lee] — "Data centers put a lot of pressure on the power grid."

### Tagalog (enrichment for a native speaker — deeper / literary)

**3. alimpuyo** [ah-leem-POO-yoh]
- **Meaning:** a whirl, swirl, or turbulent vortex — wind, water, or emotion. *Alimpuyo ng hangin* = whirlwind; *alimpuyo ng damdamin* = a turmoil of emotions.
- **Nuance:** more literary and forceful than *ipu-ipo* (a mere little whirlwind); it carries the sense of churning, building intensity — perfect for both **Super Typhoon Francisco** AND a turbulent **market** day.

**4. sapantaha** [sah-pahn-TAH-hah]
- **Meaning:** a conjecture, supposition, or educated guess; a presumption made before the facts are in.
- **Nuance:** softer and more reasoned than *haka-haka* (idle speculation) — a *sapantaha* is a considered hypothesis. Apt for traders' **sapantaha** about whether Warsh cuts rates, or whether the BSP hikes again.
