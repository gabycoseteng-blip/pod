## SEGMENT 4 — VOCAB OF THE DAY (in-language; 2 Mandarin → 2 Tagalog)
All four **new** (checked vs history ledger). Conduct Mandarin stretch in Mandarin, Tagalog stretch in Tagalog; English only as gloss.
- **Mandarin 1 (HSK-4 connective): 一旦 (yīdàn)** — "once / in the event that." Ties to Hormuz: *一旦霍尔木兹海峡关闭，油价就会飙升* ("once Hormuz closes, oil prices will spike"). Register: 书面语/analytical; pattern 一旦…就…; near-synonym contrast vs 如果 (if — neutral hypothetical) — 一旦 implies a consequential trigger you're bracing for.
- **Mandarin 2 (HSK-4 connective): 与其…不如 (yǔqí… bùrú)** — "rather than X, better to Y." Ties to capex: *与其盲目扩张，不如控制债务* ("rather than expand blindly, better to control debt"). Register: formal/argumentative; a comparative-preference frame. Contrast vs 宁可 (would rather, even at a cost).
- **Tagalog 1: pabagsak (pa-bag-SÁK)** — "downward, collapsing, a downturn/slide" (from bagsak, to fall). Ties to the chip rout / KOSPI. Contrast vs pataas (rising). Register: everyday news Tagalog. Collocation: pabagsak na presyo/merkado.
- **Tagalog 2: sagabal (sa-GA-bal)** — "an obstacle / hindrance / bottleneck." Ties to the grid: gas-turbine backlogs are a *sagabal* to the buildout. Near-synonym contrast vs hadlang (a blocking barrier) — sagabal is friction that slows you; hadlang stops you. Register: common, slightly formal.
