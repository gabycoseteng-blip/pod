## SEGMENT 4 — VOCAB OF THE DAY (~5 min) — 2 Mandarin, then 2 Tagalog
1. **反而 fǎn'ér** (3·2) — "on the contrary / instead." Advanced connective. Ties to the
   rotation: yields didn't rise, 反而 fell; tech sold off, 反而 homebuilders ripped.
   Register: neutral, leans 书面语. Collocation **不但没…反而…**. Near-synonym contrast
   vs **却 (què)**: 反而 = a result *opposite to expectation*; 却 = a milder "but."
2. **稳定 wěndìng** (3·4) — "stable / stability." Abstract collocation, finance-desk
   formal. Ties to **价格稳定** (price stability, BSP), grid stability, currency
   stability. Near-synonym contrast vs **平稳 (píngwěn)** — 稳定 = a *state* of being
   stable; 平稳 = *smooth/steady of process* (e.g. 平稳着陆, a soft landing).
3. **habagat** — the southwest monsoon (rainy-season wind). Ties to Francisco enhancing
   the habagat. Native nuance: contrast with **amihan**, the cool, dry northeast
   monsoon — habagat = hot, wet, stormy; amihan = the cool "ber-months" air.
4. **ginhawa** — relief, ease, well-being. Ties to the oil reversal giving *ginhawa* to
   the import bill and households. Native nuance vs **luwag** (literal looseness/slack,
   e.g. monetary loosening): ginhawa is the *felt* relief / well-being; luwag is the
   mechanism. Also kaginhawaan = welfare/prosperity.
