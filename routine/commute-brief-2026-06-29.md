## ENERGY / DATA CENTERS / UTILITIES (go deep)
- **The financing layer is the new story.** BIS Annual Economic Report (June 28): the
  five largest hyperscalers are on course to commit **>$1 trillion** of AI capex across
  **2025–26 combined**, **outpacing earnings and free cash flow** → pushing into **debt
  issuance**, **private credit and hedge-fund** financing with less oversight than banks.
  BIS flags **"circular financing"** — chipmakers/clouds take equity in AI labs that then
  commit to buy their chips/compute, recycling money back as revenue. Historical rhyme:
  canal mania (1830s), railway mania (1840s), 1920s electrification, dotcom.
- 2026 hyperscaler capex run-rate **$660–690B**, ~**75% (~$450B) AI-tied**.
- **Turbine throughput** (Utility-Dive/SemiAnalysis level): **GE Vernova** heavy-duty gas
  turbines — **31 ft, 280 tons, ~500k homes each**; **Microsoft bought seven → 2.7 GW**
  for a Texas data center (CNBC, June 27). OEM backlogs remain the binding constraint.
- **Behind-the-meter shift:** **Oracle's "Project Jupiter"** (New Mexico) — up to a
  **2.85 GW power block** that **replaces planned gas turbines + diesel backup with Bloom
  Energy fuel-cell Servers** — a notable fuel-cell-over-gas onsite-generation bet.
- **Index mechanics, not fundamentals:** **Bloom Energy (BE) −18%** and **Credo −20%-ish**
  Friday were driven by **Russell 2000 reconstitution removals**, not a fundamental crack
  — a clean example of forced index selling colliding with a strong secular story.
