## SEGMENT 4 — VOCAB OF THE DAY (~5 min) — 2 Mandarin, then 2 Tagalog
1. **一旦 yīdàn** (1·4) — "once / in the event that." **Advanced connective** (一旦…就…). Ties to
   the Iran ceasefire collapse: 一旦停火破裂,油价就会反弹. Register: neutral, common in analysis.
   Near-synonym vs **如果 (rúguǒ)** = plain "if": 一旦 marks a decisive turning-point condition with
   real consequences, often one you'd rather avoid; 如果 is neutral. Collocation: 一旦…就…
2. **贬值 biǎnzhí** (3·2) — "to depreciate / devalue / lose value." **Abstract finance collocation**,
   书面语/formal. Ties to the peso at a record low and the gold-as-debasement-hedge trade. Near-synonym
   vs **掉价 (diàojià)** = colloquial "drop in price / lose face": 贬值 is macro/currency/asset value
   decline (货币贬值, 大幅贬值); 掉价 is everyday. Collocation: 货币贬值, 人民币贬值.
3. **unos** (u-NÓS) — a sudden violent storm/squall; figuratively, a crisis or turmoil. Ties to
   Typhoon Inday and the market's reflation squall. Native nuance vs **bagyo** (the meteorological
   typhoon) and **sigwa** (a storm, often political/social upheaval — the First Quarter Storm was
   "Sigwa"): unos = the sudden onslaught, freely used for a financial or emotional storm.
4. **kagipitan** (ka-gi-PÍ-tan) — a tight spot; financial straits; an acute squeeze. Ties to the
   peso/import-bill bind and household budgets under imported inflation. Native nuance vs **hirap**
   (general hardship) and the adjective **gipit** (hard-up, cornered): kagipitan is the *state* of
   being in a bind — the BSP's policy kagipitan, a household's kagipitan.
