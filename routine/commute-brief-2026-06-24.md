## ENERGY / DATA CENTERS / UTILITIES (~5 min)
**The throughline pays off here: the market is repricing AI as a *power bill*, not just a stock.**
- **The live example is in France:** a record heatwave is straining the grid this week — the cleanest reminder that the system already runs with almost no margin before you add AI.
- **The price signal is screaming in PJM**, the biggest US grid operator: capacity prices have rocketed from **$28.92/MW-day (2024/25)** to **$329.17/MW-day (2026/27)** — more than a 10x jump. **Data centers drove 63%** of that increase, about **$9.3 billion** in added cost.
- **The demand curve is vertical:** Lawrence Berkeley National Lab sees US data-center electricity use roughly **doubling — from 176 TWh (2023) to 325–580 TWh by 2028.** PJM expects **peak demand +32 GW from 2024–2030, with 30 of those gigawatts from data centers** — like adding a brand-new data-center grid every year this decade.
- **It's hitting the kitchen table:** utility bills for some PJM customers are set to rise **1.5%–5%** in the 12 months starting **this month, June 2026** — which turns an AI story into a political one.
- **The supply answer leans nuclear:** at least **5 GW of dedicated data-center nuclear** is expected by 2030 — the **Three Mile Island restart**, additional reactor restarts, and the first commercial **SMRs**. Watch utilities, independent power producers, gas-turbine makers, and anything nuclear/SMR-adjacent.

## VOCAB OF THE DAY (~5 min)

### Mandarin (for the active learner — say each twice, honor the tones)

**1. 芯片 — xīnpiàn** [sheen-PYEN] (xīn = high, flat 1st tone; piàn = falling 4th tone)
- **Meaning:** semiconductor chip / microchip. (芯 xīn = core; 片 piàn = thin slice/flake.)
- **Tie-in:** today's market is all about the **芯片** — the global chip selloff and Micron's earnings tonight.
- **Example:** 这次卖盘是由芯片股带动的。 — *Zhè cì màipán shì yóu xīnpiàn gǔ dàidòng de.* [juh tsuh my-pan shr yo sheen-pyen goo dye-dong duh] — "This selloff was led by chip stocks."

**2. 加息 — jiāxī** [jyah-SHEE] (jiā = high, flat 1st tone; xī = high, flat 1st tone)
- **Meaning:** to raise interest rates / a rate hike. (加 jiā = add/increase; 息 xī = interest.) The opposite is 降息 *jiàngxī* [jyahng-shee], to cut rates.
- **Tie-in:** the whole world is doing **加息** this week — the BoJ, the ECB, the BSP, and maybe soon the Fed.
- **Example:** 多家央行同时加息。 — *Duō jiā yāngháng tóngshí jiāxī.* [dwor jyah yahng-hahng tong-shr jyah-shee] — "Several central banks are raising rates at the same time."

### Tagalog (enrichment for a native speaker — deeper / literary)

**3. daluyong** [dah-loo-YONG]
- **Meaning:** a great surge or swell — a powerful wave of water, but also of emotion or movement. *Daluyong ng alon* = a surging swell of waves; *daluyong ng damdamin* = a surge of feeling.
- **Nuance:** weightier and more sweeping than *alon* (an ordinary wave); a *daluyong* implies momentum and force gathering behind it — perfect for **Typhoon Francisco's** storm surge **and** for a market caught in a global wave of selling.

**4. agam-agam** [AH-gahm-AH-gahm]
- **Meaning:** a lingering doubt, misgiving, or quiet apprehension — that nagging feeling that something might go wrong.
- **Nuance:** softer and more inward than *takot* (fear) and more reflective than *alinlangan* (plain hesitation). *Agam-agam* is the unease that hums under the surface — exactly the investor mood right now: the **agam-agam** over whether the Fed hikes, whether AI earnings hold, whether the peso breaks 60.
