SAM: More than three years to get a transformer. And the market for this gear?
ALEX: The U.S. data-center electrical-equipment market is projected to go from about 20 billion dollars this year to 65 billion by 2030. More than triple. SAM: Both of those have consequences. Walk me through the second-order read.
ALEX: Two things. First, the financing model is quietly shifting. Second — and this is the one that should worry people — refurbishing old transformers as a bridge means you're running aging iron at exactly the interconnection points feeding the biggest new loads. That quietly degrades N-minus-1 redundancy — the grid's ability to lose one component and keep running — right where the grid is under the most stress.
SAM: So the constraint isn't the sexy stuff, the GPUs or even the turbines. It's a three-year queue for a steel box full of copper.

## SEGMENT FOUR — VOCAB OF THE DAY
ALEX: 今天我们学四个词 — 先两个中文，再两个他加禄语。(Today we'll learn four words — first two in Chinese, then two in Tagalog.) 每个都跟今天的新闻有关。(Each one is tied to today's news.)
SAM: 好，第一个词是什么？(Good — what's the first word?)
ALEX: 第一个是 "随着"。(The first is "suí zhe" — "as" or "along with.") 我给你造个句子：随着油价上涨，比索跌到了历史新低。(Let me make a sentence: As oil prices rise, the peso has fallen to a record low.)
SAM: 我懂了。所以 "随着" 表示两件事一起发生，一个跟着另一个变化。(I see — so "suí zhe" expresses two things happening together, one changing along with the other.)
ALEX: 对。它是书面语，新闻里特别常见。(Right. It's formal, written register — very common in the news.) 比如：随着战争溢价扩散，各国央行都被套住了。(For example: As the war premium spreads, central banks everywhere are boxed in.)
SAM: 那 "随着" 和 "跟着" 有什么区别？我常常搞混。(What's the difference between "suí zhe" and "gēn zhe"? I always mix them up.)
ALEX: 好问题。"跟着" 是具体地跟在后面 — 比如跟着一个人走。(Good question. "Gēn zhe" is physically following behind — like following a person as you walk.) "随着" 更抽象，表示 "随着…的变化"，通常接一个大趋势。(But "suí zhe" is more abstract — "along with the change of something" — it usually attaches to a big trend.)
SAM: 明白。搭配是 "随着…的" 加名词，对吧？像 "随着时间的推移"。(Got it — the collocation is "suí zhe … de" plus a noun, right? Like "as time goes by.")
ALEX: 完全正确。第二个词更抽象一点："导致"。(Exactly right. The second word is a bit more abstract: "dǎo zhì" — "to lead to" or "to result in.")
SAM: 意思是 "造成一个结果"？(It means "to cause a result"?)
ALEX: 对，但是要注意 — "导致" 通常接不好的结果。(Yes, but note — "dǎo zhì" usually leads to a bad result.) 比如：油价冲击导致了输入型通胀。(For example: The oil shock has led to imported inflation.) 再比如：这可能导致央行更难降息。(Or: This could make it harder for the central bank to cut rates.)
SAM: 那 "导致"、"造成"、"使得" 呢？它们差别在哪里？(What about "dǎo zhì," "zào chéng," and "shǐ dé"? Where's the difference?)
ALEX: 很好的对比。"导致" 和 "造成" 都是负面的，但 "造成" 更具体 — 造成损失，造成破坏。(Great contrast. "Dǎo zhì" and "zào chéng" are both negative, but "zào chéng" is more concrete — to cause a loss, to cause damage.) "使得" 是中性的，只是 "让某事发生"。(But "shǐ dé" is neutral — it just means "to cause something to happen.")
SAM: 所以在财经新闻里，"导致" 是最书面、最抽象的那个。(So in financial news, "dǎo zhì" is the most formal, most abstract of the three.)
ALEX: 说得对。好，现在换他加禄语。(Well put. Okay — now let's switch to Tagalog.) Lumipat tayo sa Tagalog. (Let's move to Tagalog.)
SAM: Sige. Ano ang unang salita? (Go ahead. What's the first word?)
ALEX: Ang unang salita ay "humina" — ibig sabihin, to weaken. (The first word is "humina" — meaning, to weaken.) Halimbawa: Humina ang piso hanggang umabot muli sa pinakamababang halaga nito. (For example: The peso weakened until it reached its lowest value again.)
SAM: Kaya perpekto ito para sa balita ngayon — ang piso sa record low. (So this is perfect for today's news — the peso at a record low.) Pero paano ito naiiba sa "bumagsak"? (But how is it different from "bumagsak"?)
ALEX: Magandang tanong. "Humina" ay unti-unti — dahan-dahang panghihina. (Good question. "Humina" is gradual — a slow weakening.) Pero "bumagsak" ay biglaan at malakas — parang isang pagbagsak. (But "bumagsak" is sudden and strong — like a crash.) At "lumubog" ay mas matindi pa — parang lumulubog na barko. (And "lumubog" is even more severe — like a sinking ship.)
SAM: Kaya para sa piso, "humina" ang tama — hindi ito bumagsak, unti-unti itong humina. (So for the peso, "humina" is the right one — it didn't crash, it weakened gradually.)
ALEX: Tama. Ang pangalawang salita ay "kalakalan" — trade o commerce. (Correct. The second word is "kalakalan" — trade or commerce.) Halimbawa: Ang kalakalang panlabas ang sentro ng usapan nina Rubio at Marcos. (For example: Foreign trade is the center of the Rubio–Marcos talks.)
SAM: At iba ba ito sa "negosyo"? (And is it different from "negosyo"?)
ALEX: Oo — malaki ang pagkakaiba. "Negosyo" ay isang partikular na kompanya o hanapbuhay. (Yes — a big difference. "Negosyo" is a specific company or livelihood.) Pero "kalakalan" ay ang buong sistema ng pagbili at pagbebenta — ang macro. (But "kalakalan" is the whole system of buying and selling — the macro.)
SAM: Kaya "kalakalang panlabas" — foreign trade — ang gagamitin mo para sa balance of payments at sa freight sa Bab el-Mandeb. (So "kalakalang panlabas" — foreign trade — is what you'd use for the balance of payments and the freight at Bab el-Mandeb.)
ALEX: Eksakto. Apat na salita: 随着, 导致, humina, kalakalan. (Exactly. Four words: suí zhe, dǎo zhì, humina, kalakalan.) Ang buong kuwento ng araw sa apat na salita. (The whole story of the day in four words.)
